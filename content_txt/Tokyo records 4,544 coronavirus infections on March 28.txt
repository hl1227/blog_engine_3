(AP Photo/Koji Sasahara)    TOKYO -- The Japanese capital reported 4,544 coronavirus infections on March 28, after reporting 7,844 cases the previous day, the Tokyo Metropolitan Government announced.    New case numbers typically dip on Mondays due to low testing figures over the weekend, especially on Sundays. Of the 4,544 newly infected people, 1,996 had received two COVID vaccine shots, 55 one shot, and 1,336 were unvaccinated. The vaccination status of 1,157 was unknown.    As of March 28, a total of 1,933 of Tokyo's 7,229 coronavirus-designated beds were filled, bringing the proportion of beds in use to 26.7%, while 14.4% of beds for severe patients were in use, with 116 out of 804 available beds filled. As of the same day, 483 people require oxygen administration.    There were no COVID-19 deaths reported on March 28, and total coronavirus fatalities in Tokyo remained at 4,141.    The capital is battling a sixth wave of coronavirus infections. In the first week of March, average daily infections hit 10,391.9. The daily average dipped to 8,631.9 in the second week, and 7449.1 in the third. In February, Tokyo saw a total of 416,405 new infections, averaging 14,871.6 cases per day. It registered a total of 194,563 infections in January for an average of 6,276.2 cases per day. In the entire month of December 2021, the capital saw 905 coronavirus infections, for a daily average of 29.2 cases.    Tokyo has recorded 1,225,293 infections to date, the most among Japan's 47 prefectures.    (Mainichi)
